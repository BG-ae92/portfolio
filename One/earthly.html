Earthly
Delights


Somewhere in a village
a man looks himself in a mirror.
His skin pale, worn like leather.
He raises a hand to his face
where he finds mistrust
in streaks the color cherry.

Light visited him during the early hours
when the sky was still a purplish blue
It whispered tales of misfortune
but nothing on how to stop them.

Now, as he stares himself in the mirror
the light appears again. Drifting on-
-onto the opposite side, the man reaches in
and finds himself in a valley of green,
spotted brown by the summers past.

Surrounded by those of his same complexion.
He watches in awe as they all fall
deep between the hills.

The man has seen past what man can see,
now he is left cold, reflecting
on what it means.
